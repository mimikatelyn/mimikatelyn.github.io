RESUME TIPS:

  • The resume template is a table.  You are welcome to customize this to fit your situation.  For instance, if you have a college degree, please feel free to add the degree; if you have had multiple jobs, you may include those, etc.
  • Candidates with under 10 years of work experience are encouraged to keep the resume to one page.
  • Begin each bullet with an action verb: https://resumegenius.com/blog/resume-help/action-verbs

Be sure to include accomplishment language.  To help you, consider the following:

  •  Name three accomplishments about the project that make you proud.
  •  Did you complete a particularly challenging assignment? Participate in a solution that was unique, enhanced efficiency, saved time or created a better experience for the end user?
  • Did you receive praise, recognition or pats on the back from your colleagues and teachers?
  •  Important articles that showcase how to build in accomplishments:
  • §  https://www.monster.com/career-advice/article/resume-dig-deep-accomplishments
  • §  http://www.techrepublic.com/blog/career-management/it-resume-list-accomplishments-not-responsibilities/
  • Bullets should use the following  formula: Result -> Action -> Situation to formulate your accomplishment bullets.
